What is Young Adult Panel?

Your voice in Diabetes Research

The Young Adult Panel (YAP 2.0) is a group of passionate young adults living with Type 1 diabetes who collaborate with the D1 Now research team, representing the essential voices of the patient community. This panel goes beyond participating in surveys or focus groups; it involves a long- term collaboration where members contribute to all stages of the D1 Now study. YAP members bring their unique experiences and insights to the table, helping to design studies, analyse data, and disseminate results. This collaboration ensures that the research is relevant and impactful, directly improving care for young adults living with Type 1 diabetes. Make your voice heard and help us build a better future for all those living with Type 1 diabetes.

What's Involved if You Join?

Becoming a Key Contributor

As a member of the Young Adult Panel (YAP 2.0), you'll be at the heart of the D1 Now research team, engaging in diverse activities that reflect the real needs and experiences of young adults with Type 1 diabetes.

Learn More

▶ Shape Research: Bring your unique ideas to the table, helping to design and conduct studies that truly reflect the needs of young adults with Type 1 diabetes.
▶ Make Materials Matter: Ensure patient information, consent forms, and questionnaires are clear and user-friendly, making a real difference in how young adults engage with their care.
▶ Get Hands-On with Data: Dive into data analysis, providing your insights to help interpret findings that will influence future diabetes care.
▶ Be a Voice Online: Use your creativity to manage social media channels and contribute to our website, spreading awareness and sharing our findings in engaging ways.
▶ Collaborate and Learn: Work alongside other young adults, medical staff, and researchers, gaining valuable experience in leadership, teamwork, mentoring and governance.
▶ Build Your Future: Gain practical experience in academic research, writing, and patient advocacy, boosting your resume and career prospects.
▶ Get Appreciated: Receive appreciation for your time and contributions, including participation in meetings and various activities.

You don’t have to do it all—pick the areas where you’re most passionate and can make the biggest impact. This collaboration ensures our research is not just scientifically solid but also genuinely beneficial. Your involvement will directly influence the future of diabetes care, making a significant difference in the lives of young adults with Type 1 diabetes.
